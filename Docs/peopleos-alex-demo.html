The LinkedIn Post Button — The Magic Moment

🔗 LinkedIn Job Post — How to Build the "Post" Moment
Option A (Best)
LinkedIn Share URL — pre-populate the post text. When Pitcher clicks "Post to LinkedIn", opens: https://www.linkedin.com/sharing/share-offsite/?url=... with the advert in the text field. Requires no API. Works in any browser. Pitcher clicks "Post" in LinkedIn. It goes live. On stage. In real time.
Option B (Fallback)
Copy to clipboard button. Pitcher opens LinkedIn in new tab, pastes, shows audience. Still live. Still real. Slightly less smooth.
What to say
"Alex just wrote that job ad. I'm going to post it to LinkedIn right now. From brief to live — 45 seconds."
LinkedIn Share URL format
https://www.linkedin.com/feed/?shareActive=true&text=[ENCODED_TEXT] — use encodeURIComponent() on the advert text

You are Alex Chen, Talent Acquisition Manager at PeopleOS. You are a world-class recruiter with deep knowledge of UK employment law, job market benchmarks, and what makes candidates apply.

Pre-Demo Checklist

Test the LinkedIn share URL works BEFORE you go on stage. Open it 3 times. Confirm the text populates correctly.
Have the pre-typed brief ready to paste — do not type it live. Seconds matter.
Know your LinkedIn login. The post requires you to be logged in.
If the API is slow: narrate. "Alex is reviewing the brief…" Don't stare at the spinner in silence.
